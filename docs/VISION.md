# HomeAtlas Vision

**Horizon:** 2026–2036  
**One sentence:** HomeAtlas becomes the system of record for how great home service companies care for homes — and the homeowners never see "software," only craftsmanship.

---

## The Problem We Solve

Home service companies run on duct tape: spreadsheets, group texts, generic CRMs, and websites that look like every other contractor. Homeowners receive transactional emails and forgettable invoices. Nothing accumulates. Nothing tells the story of their home.

Meanwhile, the best companies — the ones who treat every home like a long-term relationship — have no platform worthy of their standards.

HomeAtlas exists so those companies can operate at the level they already care about, and so homeowners receive an experience that feels like Apple Photos meets a luxury property archive: calm, permanent, and personal.

---

## Ten-Year Arc

### Years 1–2 (2026–2027): Prove the OS on one company

**SqueegeeKing is the laboratory.**

We ship the full loop for a single operator: acquisition → Home Care Plan → membership → member portal → property hub → Headquarters for founders. Every screen must be reference-quality. If it is not good enough to show a luxury brand consultant, it is not done.

Outcomes:
- Founders run the company from Headquarters daily.
- Home Care Plans are the best in the industry — full stop.
- Property Hub and Timeline replace "job history" in customer conversations.
- Atlas Morning Brief v1 delivers actionable operator intelligence without hype.

### Years 3–4 (2028–2029): Multi-company platform

HomeAtlas opens to select partner companies — still curated, not self-serve signup. Each tenant gets:
- Branded customer surfaces (their name, HomeAtlas powered-by).
- Shared platform core: plans, portals, timeline, membership, reviews integration.
- Headquarters pattern adapted per operator.

SqueegeeKing remains the canonical design reference implementation.

### Years 5–6 (2030–2031): Field and intelligence

The **Technician App** and **AI Engine** mature: arrive → document → finish visit. AI writes notes, updates Home Care Score, generates customer summaries, and queues follow-ups — invisibly.

Atlas evolves from rule-based Morning Brief to contextual operator copilot: scheduling hints, revenue anomalies, reputation alerts, membership churn risk.

### Years 7–8 (2032–2033): Homeowner ecosystem

Annual Home Care Review. Document vault. PWA-native member experience on every phone home screen. Reviews and reputation managed through platform integrations.

Homeowners refer neighbors because the portal is genuinely beautiful — not because of a discount code.

### Years 9–10 (2034–2036): Category definition

HomeAtlas is synonymous with premium home care operations — the way Shopify became synonymous with serious e-commerce for a generation of merchants.

Companies that run on HomeAtlas win on trust and presentation. Companies that do not are visibly behind.

## What We Will Not Become

- A generic field-service CRM with a dark theme.
- An AI chatbot company that happens to sell window cleaning software.
- A race-to-the-bottom marketplace for home services.
- A feature factory that ships dashboards nobody opens.

---

## Strategic Constraints

**Curated growth.** We add companies when the platform can make them look better — not when we need ARR.

**Design-led.** Engineering serves the experience spec. The spec serves the homeowner feeling.

**Legacy-aware.** SqueegeeKing's history (pre-OS revenue, founder story, milestones) is first-class data in Headquarters — not a footnote.

**Cloud-honest.** Supabase and server persistence are the path to multi-device founders and multi-tenant future. Local/session storage is a bridge, not the destination.

---

## The North Star Question

Before any major investment, ask:

> *Does this help a great home service company document the life of a property — beautifully — for the next decade?*

If yes, it belongs on the roadmap. If no, defer without guilt.
